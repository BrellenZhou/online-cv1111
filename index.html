Current Readings

Some say that one can know more about a person by looking at their bookshelves than having a first conversation with them. These are the books that I am currently in the middle of:

Applied Probability by Lange. I read this mostly as an exercise to remember real analysis and for the fun examples. This is the most theoretical and densest text I've ever read; I actually tried to read it in first year of undergrad, but it wasn't until my final year that I could begin to understand it. (So, that's one thing I got out of a pure math degree!)
Bayesian Data Analysis by Andrew Gelman et al. Love the balance between theory and examples in this great introduction to Bayesian statistics.
Big Data by Nathan Marz. This is a book more about introducing tools than about introducing many different ideas, so it's actually pretty light.
Beyond Good and Evil by Nietzche. So far I've only gotten to the part where he is criticizing every other philosopher out there, so it's mostly been a sleeping aid.
The Elements of Statistical Learning aka "ESL". A standard introductory text in machine learning and a great reference. Once in a while I'd sit down with one chapter and try to really thoroughly understand that concept.
Information Visualization by Colin Ware. A physiological approach to data visualization, linking how our eyes and brain work to how information should be presented. It builds visualization best practices from first principle.
The War of Art by Steven Pressfield. I've actually finished this book, but still sit down with it for a boost of motivation. Highly recommended.
